# The Philosophy of Navius

## Why it exists

Navius is not the result of a market analysis or a business opportunity. It is the result of years of frustration with navigation apps that do not respect the user.

For years I tried every GPS navigator available for Android and UbPorts. None of them gave me what I needed:

**Google Maps / Navigation** — The best at traffic calculation and destination search, but with no control over the interface, no real community alerts, and a business model built on knowing where you are at every moment of your life.

**Waze** — Active community, but owned by Google since 2013, invasive by design, and with worse traffic calculation than its parent company.

**OsmAnd / Maps.me** — Real privacy and free software, but no real-time community alerts, outdated or difficult interfaces, and missing the professional features I needed.

**uNav / Pure Maps (UbPorts)** — Respectable options for the platform, but no community alerts, no predicted traffic, and poorly smooth vehicle movement.

The conclusion was simple: the navigator I needed did not exist. So I built it.

At the same time, my frustration with the closed Android ecosystem and with living under the interests of corporations that treat users as a product led me to switch to UbPorts. Navius was born first for that system, and it is the GPS I always wanted.

---

## Core values

Navius is grounded in the values of free software as defined by the FSF. **Privacy, user control, transparency and community are not optional features: they are design principles.**

- **Privacy by design:** Routes and history stay on the device. No telemetry, no user profiles, no selling data to third parties.
- **Real control:** The user can run their own Valhalla server or work completely offline with OSM Scout. No essential feature depends on a proprietary service.
- **Open community:** Traffic alerts, speed cameras and hazards are available to all users, and anyone can contribute.
- **Transparency:** How the economic model works is explained openly. No fine print.

## Two versions, one project

Navius has a pragmatic component that must be explained honestly: **the Android version is not free software and contains advertising**. This is not a contradiction — it is a conscious decision.

### Navius for UbPorts — Free software, always free of charge

- Open source under the GPL licence
- No advertising, no subscription, no telemetry
- Funded by donations and by the revenue generated by the Android version
- This is the product that fully reflects the project's values

### Navius for Android — Proprietary, with advertising or subscription

- Proprietary code (not published)
- Non-intrusive advertising (geo-referenced billboards that never interrupt navigation)
- Optional Premium subscription to remove advertising and unlock additional features
- This is the economic engine that makes the whole project possible

### Why this model

There is a real tension between living from free software and keeping that software free. Resolving it with honesty matters more than resolving it with ideological purity.

The alternative — making the Android version completely free with no revenue source — would mean the project dies or becomes an unmaintained hobby. That serves neither UbPorts users nor anyone else.

The chosen model is transparent: UbPorts always free, because it is the platform of users who share the project's values. Android proprietary with discreet advertising, because that is where the critical mass of users is and the only viable place to generate revenue.

### What stays the same in all versions

- Routes and history stay on the device
- The community server and alerts are available to all users
- Advertising never interrupts navigation
- Location data will never be sold to advertisers

## How the project is funded

Monetisation exists to pay for the work. It does not compromise privacy. It is declared openly.

- **Donations (Liberapay):** For UbPorts users and anyone who wants to support the project directly. These fund the Valhalla server, the community server and maintenance hours.
- **Advertising on Android:** Geo-referenced billboards, non-intrusive. Navigation is never interrupted. The same billboard does not repeat within 60 seconds.
- **Premium subscription (Android):** Option to remove advertising and access additional features.

Transparency reports are published periodically with user counts, revenue by source and project costs. This builds the trust a project like this needs: the community has been repeatedly betrayed by "free" apps.

---

## Long-term vision

The goal is not just to live from Navius. It is to build a stable source of income based on free software projects that respect the user. Navius is the first. It does not have to be the last.

The model that works for independent free software developers is the **portfolio** model, not the **single product** model: several projects that sustain each other, with consulting and contracts related to the technology stack as a complement.

The immediate objective is modest: for the project to cover its costs and allow the time it deserves. The long-term objective is more ambitious: to demonstrate that useful, private and free software can be built without depending on corporations or investors.
